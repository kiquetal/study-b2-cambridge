# Study Exercises: "Every" vs. "Each"

These exercises will help you practice differentiating between "every" and "each" based on the rules we've discussed.

---

## Exercise 1: Fill in the Blanks

Fill in the blanks with **every** or **each**. Some sentences might allow for both, but consider which is *more* natural or follows the rule strictly.

1. ________ day, I wake up at 7 AM.
2. She kissed ________ of her children before they left for school.
3. The teacher gave ________ student a notebook.
4. I have two brothers; ________ of them is older than me.
5. ________ player in the team needs to sign this form.
6. She was carrying a heavy bag in ________ hand.

---

## Exercise 2: Identify the Errors

Identify and correct the errors in the following sentences. If a sentence is correct, write "Correct".

1. Every of the students passed the exam.
2. I have two eyes, and I can see clearly with every eye.
3. The guests had a drink. Each was offered a snack as well.
4. Every of these cakes is delicious.

---

## Exercise 3: Explain the Difference

In the following pairs of sentences, explain the difference in emphasis or focus (Group vs. Individual).

1. 
   A: **Every** employee attended the meeting.
   B: **Each** employee attended the meeting.

---

## Exercise 4: Final Challenge (Mixed)

Fill in the blanks with **every** or **each**.

1. ________ of the two candidates has a different strategy.
2. I try to exercise ________ morning before work.
3. The prize money was divided equally; ________ received $500.
4. I have seen ________ of these movies at least twice.
5. ________ person in the room started clapping when she finished.

---

## Answer Key for Exercise 4

1. **Each** (Used with "of" and "two").
2. **Every** (General).
3. **Each** (Used as a pronoun standing alone).
4. **Each** (Used with "of").
5. **Every** (General).
