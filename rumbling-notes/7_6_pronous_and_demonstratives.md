# 7.6 Impersonal Pronouns (it, there) and Demonstratives

This section covers the use of the impersonal pronouns it and there, which are essential for forming many common English sentences. It also touches on demonstratives.

***

## 1. Using It

The pronoun `it` has several important functions. It can refer to a specific thing, but it is also frequently used as a "dummy" or "empty" subject in sentences where there is no natural subject.

***

### A. Basic Uses of It

1.  **To refer to a thing, action, or situation previously mentioned:**
    *   "I bought a new phone. **It** is much faster than my old one."
    *   "The train was delayed. **It** made me late for my appointment."

2.  **To talk about weather, time, and distance:**
    *   **Weather:** "**It** is raining." / "**It** will be sunny tomorrow."
    *   **Time:** "**It** is half past ten." / "**It**'s time to go."
    *   **Distance:** "**It** is five miles to the next town."

---

### B. It as a Dummy Subject with Adjectives

These patterns are very common for expressing opinions or stating facts.

1.  **`it + be + adjective + to-infinitive`**
    *   "**It is important to be** on time."
    *   "**It was difficult to understand** what he was saying."
    *   *Note:* The `-ing` form (gerund) can also be used here: "**It was nice talking** to you."

2.  **`it + be + adjective + for someone + to-infinitive`**
    *   "**It is important for everyone to be** on time."
    *   "**It will be difficult for him to understand** the new rules."

3.  **`it + be + adjective + that + [subject + verb]`**
    *   "**It is essential that you finish** the report by Friday."
    *   "**It's a shame that they couldn't** come to the party."

***

### C. Using It with the Verb Find

The structure `find + it + adjective` is used to express a personal feeling or attitude towards something.

---

1.  **`find + it + adjective + to-infinitive`**
    *   "I **find it fascinating to learn** about other cultures."
    *   "She **finds it difficult to speak** in public."

2.  **`find + object + adjective`** (without `it`)
    *   "I **found the movie boring**."
    *   "He **finds his new job stressful**."

***

### D. Using It for Emphasis (Cleft Sentences)

The structure `It + be + [emphasized part] + that/who...` is used to emphasize a specific part of a sentence.
*   *Normal sentence:* "John broke the window."
*   *Emphasizing John:* "**It was John who** broke the window."
*   *Emphasizing the window:* "**It was the window that** John broke."

***

## 2. Using There

`There` is used to state that something exists or happens. The real subject of the sentence comes *after* the verb `be`.

1.  **`There + be + noun/noun phrase`**
    *   **Singular:** "**There is a cat** in the garden."
    *   **Plural:** "**There are three cats** in the garden."
    *   **Uncountable:** "**There is some milk** in the fridge."

---

### More on Using There

2.  **`There + be + number + of + pronoun`**
    *   "**There are five of us** working on this project."
    *   "How many are coming?" "**There will be ten of them** in total."

3.  **`There + modal verb + be...`**
    *   "**There must be** a mistake."
    *   "**There might be** some tickets left."
    *   "**There were three cars parked** outside."

***

## 3. Demonstratives (this, that, these, those)

Demonstratives point to specific people or things based on **number** and **distance**.

**Summary:**
*   **Near (here):** `this` (singular), `these` (plural)
*   **Far (there):** `that` (singular), `those` (plural)

***

### A. As Determiners (before a noun)

*   "Could you pass me **this book**?" (A single book, here).
*   "What is **that building** over there?" (A single building, far away).
*   "I haven't seen **these photos** before." (Multiple photos, here).
*   "**Those cars** are very expensive." (Multiple cars, far away).

---

### B. As Pronouns (replacing a noun)

*   "**This** is my house." (Pointing to the house you are at).
*   "**That** looks delicious!" (Referring to food on the other side of the table).
*   "**These** are my friends, John and Jane." (Introducing people next to you).
*   "**Those** were the days." (Referring to a time in the past).

***

### C. Other Common Uses

1.  **On the telephone:**
    *   "Hello, **this** is Maria."

2.  **Referring to time:**
    *   `this` / `these`: Current or upcoming period.
        *   "I'm very busy **this week**."
    *   `that` / `those`: Past or finished period.
        *   "I was on holiday **that week**."
        *   "Ah, 1999... **those were the days**!"
