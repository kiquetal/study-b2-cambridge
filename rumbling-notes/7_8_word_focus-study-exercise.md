# Phrasal Verbs: Get & Set - Explanations and Exercises

Here are the explanations of the phrasal verbs listed in your notes, followed by how they apply to the specific sentences you provided.

## Explanations of Phrasal Verbs

*   **Get away (with something):** To escape blame or punishment when you do something wrong. (Note: just "get away" means to escape or go on vacation).
*   **Get someone down:** To make someone feel depressed or unhappy.
*   **Get on with (someone):** To have a friendly relationship with someone.
*   **Get on with (something):** To continue doing something, especially to make progress or after an interruption.
*   **Get over (something/someone):** To recover from an illness, a bad experience, or a breakup.
*   **Get something over with:** To do and complete something difficult or unpleasant that you have to do, so you don't have to worry about it anymore.
*   **Get round to (something / doing something):** To find the time to do something that you have intended to do for a long time. *(Grammar note: always followed by a noun or a verb ending in -ing).*
*   **Get to:** To arrive at a place.

---

## Answer Key

Now, let's see which phrasal verb fits the meaning of each sentence you provided:

**1. He has a friendly relationship with his girlfriend's family**
*   *Answer:* He **gets on with** his girlfriend's family.

**2. The job made him unhappy, so he left**
*   *Answer:* The job was **getting him down**, so he left.

**3. She wanted to complete the task as quickly as possible.**
*   *Answer:* She wanted to **get the task over with** as quickly as possible.

**4. After a few days, he recovered from the illness**
*   *Answer:* After a few days, he **got over** the illness.

**5. I haven't found time for writing to them yet**
*   *Answer:* I haven't **got round to** writing to them yet.

**6. We arrived at the airport just before the flight left**
*   *Answer:* We **got to** the airport just before the flight left.

**7. I needed to make progress with my work so that I would be ready in time**
*   *Answer:* I needed to **get on with** my work so that I would be ready in time.

**8. People shouldn't escape punishment for doing such terrible things.**
*   *Answer:* People shouldn't **get away with** doing such terrible things.

## 💡 Nice to Know (B2 Tips)
*   **Get on with** has two distinct meanings: one for relationships (getting on with *someone*) and one for tasks (getting on with *something*). Both are very common in the B2 First (FCE) exam.
*   **Get round to** is a classic B2 phrasal verb that is frequently tested in 'Key Word Transformations'. Remember it is always followed by a gerund (`-ing`), e.g., "I haven't got round to **cleaning** the car."
*   **Get away vs. Get away with**: "Get away" simply means to escape or go on vacation. To express escaping punishment (as in sentence 8), we must add "with" -> **get away with**.
