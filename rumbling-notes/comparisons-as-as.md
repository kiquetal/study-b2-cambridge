# Comparisons with "as ... as"

We use the structure **"as + [adjective/adverb] + as"** to compare two things, people, or actions.

## 1. Equality (Things are the same)
When two things are equal, we use "as + adjective + as".
- *The film is as interesting as the book.*
- *He is as tall as his father.*

## 2. Inequality (Negative: Things are not the same)
When we want to say one thing is less than another, we use "not as + adjective + as". 
*Note: You can also use "not so + adjective + as", though "as ... as" is more common.*
- *This house is not as big as our old one.*
- *I am not as interested in politics as you are.*

## 3. The "as ... as" Structure with Adverbs
You can also use this with adverbs to describe how something is done.
- *He doesn't run as quickly as he used to.*

## 4. Key Rules to Remember
- **Keep it simple:** The adjective remains in its base form. Do NOT use comparatives like "bigger" or "more interesting" inside the "as...as" structure.
  - *Incorrect:* She is as taller as her sister.
  - *Correct:* She is as tall as her sister.
- **The Pronoun/Verb:** When comparing, we often end the sentence with a pronoun (me/you/him) or a subject + auxiliary verb (you are, he does).
  - *She is as fast as him.* (Informal)
  - *She is as fast as he is.* (Formal/Complete)
