# Relative Clauses: Defining vs. Non-Defining

Relative clauses add information about a noun. There are two types:

## 1. Defining Relative Clauses
These provide **essential information** that identifies the noun. If you remove the clause, the sentence loses its meaning or becomes ambiguous.
- **Rules:**
  - **NO commas** are used.
  - You **can** use "that" instead of "who" or "which".
  - You **can omit** the relative pronoun (who, which, that) if it is the object of the clause.
- *Example:* "The man **who lives next door** is a doctor." (We need to know *which* man we are talking about).

## 2. Non-Defining Relative Clauses
These provide **extra information** that is not essential to identify the person or thing.
- **Rules:**
  - **Commas MUST be used** (before and after the clause).
  - You **CANNOT use "that"**.
  - You **CANNOT omit** the relative pronoun.
- *Example:* "My brother, **who lives in London**, is a doctor." (We know who the brother is; the fact that he lives in London is just extra info).

## Quick Comparison Table
| Feature | Defining | Non-Defining |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Commas?** | No | Yes |
| **Use 'that'?** | Yes | No |
| **Omit pronoun?** | Yes (if object) | No |
| **Essential?** | Yes | No |
