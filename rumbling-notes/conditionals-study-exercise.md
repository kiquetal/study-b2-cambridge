# Exercises: Conditionals

## Exercise 1: Mixed Conditionals
Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verbs in brackets.

1.  If I _______________ (not / spend) all my money yesterday, I _______________ (have) enough to go to the cinema tonight.
2.  If she _______________ (be) more organized, she _______________ (not / forget) the meeting last Tuesday.
3.  We _______________ (be) lost now if we _______________ (not / bring) a map.
4.  If they _______________ (listen) to the advice I gave them, they _______________ (not / be) in this mess now.
5.  I _______________ (can) help you with your homework if I _______________ (not / be) so busy this week.

## Exercise 2: Key Word Transformation (B2 First Style)
Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first sentence, using the word given. **Do not change the word given.** You must use between two and five words, including the word given.

1.  I didn't see the hole, so I fell in.
    **SEEN**
    If I _________________________________ the hole, I wouldn't have fallen in.

2.  I'm not you, but I think you should apologize.
    **WERE**
    If _________________________________ , I would apologize.

3.  I'll only come to the party if you go too.
    **UNLESS**
    I won't come to the party _________________________________ too.

4.  I forgot my phone, so I couldn't call you.
    **HAD**
    If I _________________________________ my phone, I could have called you.

5.  He is very rich because he won the lottery last year.
    **WON**
    If he _________________________________ the lottery last year, he wouldn't be so rich now.

## Exercise 3: Choose the Correct Word
Choose the correct option to complete each sentence.

1.  I'll take a coat **in case / if** it gets cold later.
2.  **Unless / As long as** you study hard, you will pass the exam.
3.  I'll lend you the book **provided / even if** you promise to bring it back.
4.  **If / In case** I see him, I'll tell him you called.
5.  I wouldn't go there **even if / as long as** they paid me!

## 💡 Nice to Know (B2 Tips)
*   **Wish and If only:** These are very similar to second and third conditionals.
    *   `I wish I had...` (Present regret -> 2nd)
    *   `I wish I had had...` (Past regret -> 3rd)
*   **Should** in conditionals (formal): "If you should see him..." = "If you happen to see him..."
*   **Were to** (formal): "If I were to tell you the truth, would you believe me?" (emphasizes the hypothetical nature).
