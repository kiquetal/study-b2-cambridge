# Exercises: Comparisons with "as ... as"

## Part 1: Fill in the Blanks
Complete the sentences using "as ... as" or "not as ... as" with the adjective provided.

1. This coffee is ___________ (strong) the one I had this morning.
2. My brother is ___________ (tall) my father.
3. This task is ___________ (difficult) I thought it would be.
4. She is ___________ (fast) her sister.
5. Living here is ___________ (expensive) living in the city center.

## Part 2: Sentence Correction
Identify and correct the mistake in each sentence.

1. It is as more cold as it was yesterday.
2. He is not so fast as me, but he is as tall than me.
3. The train is as quicklier as the bus.

## Part 3: Rewrite the Sentences
Rewrite these sentences using "as ... as" to keep the same meaning.

1. The red car is faster than the blue car. (The blue car...)
2. My laptop is better than yours. (Your laptop...)
3. I am more interested in music than my friend is. (My friend...)

## Part 4: Using Set Expressions
Fill in the blanks with the correct expression: *as soon as possible*, *as much as I can*, *as far as I know*, or *as long as*.

1. I will finish the report ___________. I promise!
2. You can stay here ___________ you keep quiet.
3. ___________, the store closes at 9 PM.
4. I will try to help you ___________, but I have a lot of work today.

***

## Answer Key
### Part 1
1. as strong as
2. as tall as
3. not as difficult as
4. as fast as
5. not as expensive as

### Part 2
1. "It is as cold as..." (Do not use comparative "more cold")
2. "...but he is as tall as me." (Do not use "than")
3. "The train is as quick as..." (Do not use comparative "quicklier")

### Part 3
1. The blue car is not as fast as the red car.
2. Your laptop is not as good as mine.
3. My friend is not as interested in music as I am.

### Part 4
1. as soon as possible
2. as long as
3. As far as I know
4. as much as I can
