# Understanding the Order of Adjectives (B2 Level)

When using multiple adjectives before a noun in English, they usually follow a specific order. This order isn't a strict grammatical rule that will make your sentence incorrect if you deviate, but it sounds much more natural and fluent when followed.

---

### The Mnemonic: OSASCOMP

To help you remember the common order, we can use the mnemonic **OSASCOMP**.

Let's break down what each letter stands for:

*   **O** - **Opinion:** Describes what you think about something (e.g., *beautiful, lovely, ugly, delicious, interesting*).
*   **S** - **Size:** Describes how big or small something is (e.g., *huge, tiny, small, large, enormous*).
*   **A** - **Age:** Describes how old something is (e.g., *old, new, antique, ancient, young, modern*).
*   **S** - **Shape:** Describes the shape of something (e.g., *round, square, triangular, oval, rectangular*).
*   **C** - **Color:** Describes the color of something (e.g., *red, blue, green, black, white, golden*).
*   **O** - **Origin:** Describes where something comes from (e.g., *French, American, Japanese, Italian, lunar*).
*   **M** - **Material:** Describes what something is made of (e.g., *wooden, metallic, plastic, cotton, silk, leather*).
*   **P** - **Purpose:** Describes what something is used for (often ends in -ing, e.g., *sleeping bag, writing desk, running shoes*).

---

***

### Detailed Explanation and Examples

1.  **Opinion Adjectives** (What you think)
    *   These always come first because they are subjective.
    *   Examples: *a **beautiful** old house, a **delicious** hot meal, an **interesting** ancient artifact.*

2.  **Size Adjectives** (How big or small)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful **large** old house, a delicious **small** hot meal.*

---

3.  **Age Adjectives** (How old)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large **old** house, an interesting **ancient** artifact.*

4.  **Shape Adjectives** (What shape)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large old **square** house, a tiny **round** blue ball.*

5.  **Color Adjectives** (What color)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large old square **red** house, a tiny round **blue** ball.*

6.  **Origin Adjectives** (Where from)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large old square red **French** house, a tiny round blue **plastic** ball.*

---

7.  **Material Adjectives** (What made of)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large old square red French **stone** house, a tiny round blue plastic **toy** ball.*

8.  **Purpose Adjectives** (What for)
    *   Examples: *a beautiful large old square red French stone **family** home, a comfy **sleeping** bag.*

***

### Putting it all together (OSASCOMP Order):

**Opinion - Size - Age - Shape - Color - Origin - Material - Purpose - NOUN**

**Example:**

*   "She bought a **beautiful (O) small (S) old (A) round (S) red (C) French (O) wooden (M) coffee (P)** table."

---

**Important Notes:**

*   It's rare to use more than three or four adjectives before a single noun. Using too many can make your sentence sound clunky.
*   If you have two adjectives from the same category (e.g., two opinion adjectives like "lovely, charming"), you would usually separate them with "and" or a comma: "a lovely **and** charming dog" or "a lovely, charming dog."
*   Numbers or determiners (e.g., *two, many, a, the*) always come *before* any adjectives.

---

### Practice Tip:

Try to describe objects around you using 2-3 adjectives, focusing on applying the OSASCOMP order. The more you practice, the more natural it will feel!
