### Nice to Know Tips:

*   **Determiners:** Words like *a, an, the, my, your, two, many, some* always come *before* any adjectives. Example: *"**The** beautiful old French car."*

---

*   **Commas:** You generally don't use commas between adjectives in the standard OSASCOMP order, especially if they are from different categories. You might use a comma if two adjectives are from the same category and could be interchangeable (e.g., "a charming, lovely girl"), but it's less common with the strict OSASCOMP sequence.

---

*   **Emphasis:** Sometimes, for emphasis or stylistic reasons, native speakers might slightly alter the order, but for clarity and naturalness, sticking to OSASCOMP is the best approach for B2 learners.
*   **Practise with everyday objects:** Look around your room and try to describe 3-4 objects using multiple adjectives. Say the descriptions out loud to get a feel for the natural flow.
