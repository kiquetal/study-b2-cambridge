# Study Exercises: Determiners and Pronouns (B2 Level)

These exercises will help you practice and consolidate your understanding of determiners and pronouns like `one`, `ones`, `each`, `every`, `both`, `either`, and `neither`.

---

## Exercise 1: Choose the correct word (`one`, `ones`, `a/an`)

Complete the following sentences with `one`, `ones`, `a/an`, or `the one(s)` where appropriate.

1.  I need a new pen. Can you lend me ______? (singular, general)
2.  My shoes are old. I need to buy some new _______. (plural, general)
3.  Which car do you prefer? ______ with the red roof. (singular, specific)
4.  These books are too heavy. I need lighter _______. (plural, general)
5.  I don't have a car, but my brother has _______. (singular, general)
6.  The blue dress is nice, but I think I'll go for ______ with the flowers. (singular, specific)
7.  I don't like these apples. Do you have any sweeter _______? (plural, general)
8.  He was ______ of the most talented students in the class. (singular, part of a group)

---

## Exercise 2: Each vs. Every

Choose the correct word (`each` or `every`) to complete the sentences. Explain your choice for a few sentences.

1.  There were two main candidates, and _______ of them had strong arguments.
2.  _______ person in the room received a badge.
3.  The teacher gave _______ student a different task.
4.  _______ morning, I wake up at 7 AM.
5.  In a tennis match, _______ player tries their best to win.
6.  He visits his grandparents _______ summer.
7.  We spoke to _______ committee member individually.

## Exercise 4: Error Correction

Find and correct the mistakes in the following sentences. Some sentences may be correct.

1.  Every of the students passed the exam.
2.  I have two cats, and both them are black.
3.  She offered me two options, but I liked neither one.
4.  Could you give me an bigger one?
5.  We both went to the party.
6.  The company has two branches, and either branch offers good service.
7.  Each person has their own opinion.
8.  I need to buy new glasses, my old ones are broken.
