# Expressing Preference: "I prefer," "I would rather," and "I would prefer"

At a B2 level, it is important to know how to express preferences correctly using different grammatical structures.

## 1. "I prefer" (General Preference)
We use "prefer" to talk about general likes or preferences.
*   **Structure:** `prefer + noun` or `prefer + -ing` OR `prefer + to + infinitive`
*   **Example:** "I **prefer coffee** to tea."
*   **Example:** "I **prefer working** from home."
*   **Example:** "I **prefer to cycle** to work."

---

## 2. "I would prefer" (Specific Preference)
This is used for a specific choice in a specific situation. It is slightly more formal than "would rather."
*   **Structure:** `would prefer + to + infinitive`
*   **Example:** "I **would prefer to go** by train tonight."
*   **Note:** If you want to compare two things, use "rather than": "I would prefer to eat at home **rather than go** to a restaurant."

---

## 3. "I would rather" (Specific Preference)
"Would rather" is very common in spoken English. It is followed by the bare infinitive (infinitive without "to").
*   **Structure:** `would rather + base verb`
*   **Example:** "I **would rather stay** home tonight."
*   **Comparing:** "I **would rather eat** pizza **than** burger."

---

## Summary Table

| Expression | Structure | Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I prefer** | `+ noun` or `+ -ing` | General preferences/habits. |
| **I would prefer** | `+ to + infinitive` | Specific choice, slightly formal. |
| **I would rather** | `+ base verb` | Specific choice, very common/informal. |

---

## Key Traps to Avoid

1.  **"Would rather" never uses "to":**
    *   *Correct:* "I would rather **go**."
    *   *Incorrect:* "I would rather **to go**."
2.  **"Prefer" uses "to," not "than":**
    *   *Correct:* "I prefer coffee **to** tea."
    *   *Incorrect:* "I prefer coffee **than** tea."
3.  **Mixing structures:**
    *   Don't say "I would prefer go." It must be "I would prefer **to** go."
