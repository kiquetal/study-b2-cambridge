# 7.3 Pronouns: Possessives, Reflexives & More

This document provides a comprehensive summary of personal, possessive, reflexive, and other types of pronouns, along with structured exercises suitable for B2 level practice.

### I. Core Concepts & Summary

#### Summary Table: Pronouns and Possessives

| Type                     | Function                                | Examples (Singular)   | Examples (Plural)      | Key Usage Rule                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :----------------------- | :-------------------------------------- | :-------------------- | :--------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Personal Pronouns**    |                                         |                       |                        |                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Subject                  | Perform the action of the verb          | I, you, he, she, it   | we, you, they          | Always come before the main verb (unless it's a question).                                                                                                                                 |
| Object                   | Receive the action of the verb or follow a preposition | me, you, him, her, it | us, you, them          | Always come after the main verb or after a preposition. The object form is also used after the verb 'be' (e.g., "It was *him*.").                                                       |
| **Possessive Adjectives**| Show ownership, must be followed by a noun | my, your, his, her, its | our, your, their       | *Must always be followed by a noun.* E.g., `my car`. Can be used with `own` for emphasis: `my own car`.                                                                                   |
| **Possessive Pronouns**  | Replace a possessive adjective + noun, stand alone | mine, yours, his, hers, its* | ours, yours, theirs    | *Stand alone* and act as a noun. E.g., "That car is `mine`." Used in the structure: `a friend of mine`.                                                                               |
| **Indefinite Pronouns**  | Refer to unspecified people, places, things | somebody, everyone, anything, nobody | (Same forms often)     | `some-` in positive sentences. `any-` in negative statements & most questions. `no-` with a positive verb.                                                                          |
| **Reflexive Pronouns**   | Subject performs action on itself       | myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself | ourselves, yourselves, themselves | Subject and object are the same. E.g., "He cut `himself`." Used after `enjoy`. Not used with: `relax`, `concentrate`, `afford`.                                                          |
| **Emphatic Pronouns**    | Emphasize the subject                   | myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself | ourselves, yourselves, themselves | Appears right after the subject or at the end of the sentence. E.g., "I `myself` did it."                                                                                             |
| **Reciprocal Pronouns**  | Express mutual action/relationship      | N/A                   | each other, one another | `each other` for two entities; `one another` for three or more (though this distinction is becoming less strict).                                                                             |

*Note: `its` as a possessive pronoun is very rare.*

#### Possessive 's for Nouns
*   **Singular Noun/Name:** Add `'s` (e.g., `the cat's toy`, `James's book`).
*   **Plural Noun ending in -s:** Add `’` (e.g., `the students' desks`).
*   **Plural Noun not ending in -s:** Add `'s` (e.g., `the children's games`).
