# Exercises: Defining vs. Non-Defining Relative Clauses

## Part 1: Identification
Decide if the relative clause in each sentence is **Defining (D)** or **Non-Defining (ND)**. Add commas where necessary.

1. The car which I bought last week has broken down.
2. My car which I bought last week has broken down.
3. The people who live in that house are very friendly.
4. My aunt who lives in that house is very friendly.
5. The book that you lent me is very interesting.

## Part 2: Transformation
Rewrite the two sentences into one using a relative clause. Decide if you need commas.

1. I visited Paris. It is the capital of France.
2. The woman is waiting outside. She wants to see you.
3. My new phone is broken. I bought it yesterday.
4. The man is a famous actor. You met him at the party.

***

## Answer Key

### Part 1
1. **D** (No commas needed).
2. **ND** -> *My car, which I bought last week, has broken down.*
3. **D** (No commas needed).
4. **ND** -> *My aunt, who lives in that house, is very friendly.*
5. **D** (No commas needed).

### Part 2
1. *Paris, which is the capital of France, is a city I visited.* (ND)
2. *The woman who/that is waiting outside wants to see you.* (D)
3. *My new phone, which I bought yesterday, is broken.* (ND)
4. *The man who/that you met at the party is a famous actor.* (D)
