# Differentiating Between "Every" and "Each"

Here is a breakdown of the difference between **every** and **each**.

Imagine you are looking at a group of students.

*   If you say, "**Every** student passed the exam," you are thinking of the students as a **whole group**. You are making a general statement about all of them together. It’s like taking a group photo.
*   If you say, "**Each** student received a different grade," you are thinking of the students as **individuals**, one by one. You are highlighting their separateness. It’s like looking at individual portraits of each student.

Here is a table to summarize the key differences:

| Feature | **Every** | **Each** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Idea** | Refers to all members of a group as a whole. Emphasizes "all of them." | Refers to the individual members of a group, one by one. Emphasizes "one by one." |
| **Number** | Used for **three or more** items/people. | Used for **two or more** items/people. |
| **Grammar** | Always followed by a singular noun.<br>e.g., `every student` | Can be followed by a singular noun, or used with "of".<br>e.g., `each student`, `each of the students` |
| **As a Pronoun** | Cannot stand alone. | Can stand alone as a pronoun.<br>e.g., "The students got a prize. **Each** was happy." |

---

### Examples

#### 1. Number of Items (A key rule)

This is the easiest way to decide. If there are only two, you **must** use **each**.

*   She was holding a cup in **each** hand. (Correct - we have two hands)
*   ~~She was holding a cup in~~ **~~every~~** ~~hand.~~ (Incorrect)

#### 2. Grammar: Using "of" and Pronouns

*   **Each of** the players has a specific role. (Correct)
*   ~~**Every of** the players has a specific role.~~ (Incorrect - you can never say "every of")
*   The tour guide gave the tourists a map. He gave one to **each**. (Correct - "each" can stand alone)
*   ~~...He gave one to~~ **~~every~~**~~.~~ (Incorrect)

#### 3. Emphasis and Meaning

Sometimes, both are grammatically correct, but there's a subtle difference in meaning.

*   **Every** car in this car park is new.
    *   (Meaning: All the cars, as a complete set, are new. It's a general observation about the group.)
*   **Each** car in this car park has a different owner.
    *   (Meaning: We are thinking about the cars individually. Car 1 has an owner, Car 2 has an owner, etc.)

---

### Quick Test to Help You Choose

Ask yourself these questions when you are unsure:

1.  **Is the group size only two?**
    *   If yes, you **must** use **each**.
2.  **Am I thinking of the items as one complete group?**
    *   If yes, **every** is probably better.
3.  **Am I thinking of the items one by one, separately?**
    *   If yes, **each** is probably better.
4.  **Do I need to use "of the..."?**
    *   If yes, you **must** use **each**.
