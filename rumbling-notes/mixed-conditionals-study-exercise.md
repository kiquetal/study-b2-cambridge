# Mixed Conditionals - Study Exercises

1.  If I (to know) about the traffic, I (to leave) earlier and (to be) here by now.
2.  If she (to be) more careful, she (not to break) her arm yesterday.
3.  If they (to accept) the job offer, they (to live) in London now.
4.  If he (not to be) so shy, he (to talk) to her at the party last night.

## Exercise 3: Identify and correct the mistakes in the following mixed conditional sentences.

1.  If I would have known you were coming, I would prepare dinner.
2.  If she wasn't so busy, she would have gone to the party last night.
3.  If they didn't have a car, they wouldn't had gone on that road trip.
4.  If he was a doctor, he would have helped her immediately yesterday.
5.  If you had listened to me, you wouldn't be in this mess today.

## Answer Key (For self-correction)

### Exercise 1:

1.  If I **had known** about the traffic, I **would have left** earlier and **would be** here by now.
2.  If she **were** more careful, she **wouldn't have broken** her arm yesterday.
3.  If they **had accepted** the job offer, they **would be living** in London now.
4.  If he **weren't** so shy, he **would have talked** to her at the party last night.
5.  If we **had saved** more money, we **could buy** a new car next month.
6.  If I **spoke** Italian, I **would have understood** what they said during the meeting.
7.  If you **had told** me about your problem, I **would have helped** you.
8.  If he **had finished** his degree, he **wouldn't be working** as a waiter now.
9.  If I **were** braver, I **would have asked** her to dance at the ball.
10. If she **had taken** the medicine, she **would be feeling** better today.

### Exercise 2:

1.  If he **had saved** money, he **could afford** a holiday now.
2.  If I **were** good at math, I **wouldn't have failed** the exam last week.
3.  If she **had listened** to my advice, she **wouldn't be** in trouble now.
4.  If they **were** interested in history, they **would have visited** the museum when they were in Rome.
5.  If he **had gotten** the promotion, he **wouldn't feel** demotivated now.

### Exercise 3:

1.  If I **had known** you were coming, I **would have prepared** dinner. (Original: If I would have known you were coming, I would prepare dinner.)
2.  **Correct.** (If she wasn't so busy, she would have gone to the party last night.) - *This is a Type 2 + Type 3 mixed conditional.*
3.  If they **didn't have** a car, they **wouldn't have gone** on that road trip. (Original: If they didn't have a car, they wouldn't had gone on that road trip.)
4.  **Correct.** (If he was a doctor, he would have helped her immediately yesterday.) - *This is a Type 2 + Type 3 mixed conditional, implying a general characteristic affecting a past action.*
5.  **Correct.** (If you had listened to me, you wouldn't be in this mess today.) - *This is a Type 3 + Type 2 mixed conditional.)
