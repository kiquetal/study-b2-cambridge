# Mixed Conditionals

Mixed conditionals combine different conditional types (Type 1, 2, or 3) in one sentence to express hypothetical situations that have a past condition and a present result, or a present condition and a past result. They are used when the time frame of the 'if' clause is different from the time frame of the main clause.

## Type 3 + Type 2 (Past condition, present result)

This is the most common type of mixed conditional. It describes a hypothetical situation in the past that has a result in the present.

*   **Structure:** If + Past Perfect (Type 3), would/could/might + base verb (Type 2)
*   **Meaning:** If something had been different in the past, something would be different now.

**Examples:**
*   **If I had studied harder (past condition), I would have a better job now (present result).**
    (Meaning: I didn't study hard in the past, so I don't have a better job now.)
*   **If she hadn't missed the flight (past condition), she would be on vacation right now (present result).**
    (Meaning: She missed the flight, so she isn't on vacation now.)
*   **If we had taken that advice (past condition), we wouldn't be in this difficult situation (present result).**
    (Meaning: We didn't take that advice, so we are in a difficult situation now.)

## Type 2 + Type 3 (Present condition, past result - Less common)

This type describes a hypothetical situation in the present that had a result in the past. This is less common and often implies a present characteristic or habit affecting a past outcome.

*   **Structure:** If + Simple Past (Type 2), would have + past participle (Type 3)
*   **Meaning:** If something were different now (or generally), something would have been different in the past.

**Examples:**
*   **If I weren't afraid of heights (present condition), I would have gone skydiving with them last year (past result).**
    (Meaning: I am afraid of heights now, so I didn't go skydiving then.)
*   **If he spoke French (present condition), he would have understood what they said yesterday (past result).**
    (Meaning: He doesn't speak French now, so he didn't understand yesterday.)
*   **If she were more organized (present condition), she wouldn't have lost her keys so many times (past result).**
    (Meaning: She isn't organized now, so she lost her keys many times.)
