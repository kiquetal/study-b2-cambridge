# Reported Speech: Advanced Reporting Verbs

In B2 First, you are expected to move beyond "said" and "told." The difficulty lies in the **grammatical structure** that follows the reporting verb.

## 1. Verb + -ing (Gerund)
Some verbs must be followed directly by the -ing form.
*   **Suggest:** "He **suggested going** to the cinema." (NOT *suggested to go*)
*   **Deny:** "She **denied stealing** the money."
*   **Recommend:** "I **recommend visiting** the museum."
*   **Admit:** "He **admitted breaking** the vase."

## 2. Verb + Preposition + -ing
*   **Apologize for:** "He **apologized for being** late."
*   **Insist on:** "They **insisted on paying** for dinner."
*   **Accuse someone of:** "She **accused him of lying**."
*   **Congratulate someone on:** "I **congratulated her on passing** her exam."

## 3. Verb + Object + To-Infinitive
*   **Remind:** "She **reminded me to lock** the door."
*   **Persuade:** "I **persuaded him to come** with us."
*   **Warn:** "They **warned us not to go** near the edge."
*   **Encourage:** "He **encouraged me to apply** for the job."

## 4. Passive Reporting (The "It is said" structure)
Used for general opinions or facts.
*   **Structure A:** `It + passive verb + that + clause`
    *   "**It is thought that** the company is in trouble."
*   **Structure B:** `Subject + passive verb + to-infinitive`
    *   "**The company is thought to be** in trouble."

## 5. Reporting Questions (The Word Order Trap)
When reporting a question, we use **statement word order** (Subject + Verb) and NO "do/does/did."
*   **Direct:** "Where is the station?"
*   **Reported:** "He asked me **where the station was**." (NOT *where was the station*)
*   **Yes/No questions:** Use **if** or **whether**.
    *   "Do you like coffee?" -> "He asked me **if I liked** coffee."
