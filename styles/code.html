Technical Framing: Clause Integration

Structure 7.42 dictates the synchronization of Relative Clauses within non-defining frameworks. In B2 Cambridge protocols, these must be isolated via punctuation markers (commas) to prevent semantic overflow into the core mission statement.

Operational Guidelines

Defining Clauses

Essential information that identifies which person or thing we are talking about. Removing these would make the sentence incomplete or confusing.

INPUT: "The astronaut who repaired the shuttle is a hero."
RESULT: Defining (No commas)

Non-Defining Clauses

Provides extra information that is not critical to identifying the subject. Always requires punctuation buffers.

INPUT: "The shuttle, which was built in 1998, is being retired."
RESULT: Non-Defining (Comma required)

Critical Exam Note

In formal B2 Cambridge writing, avoid using "that" in non-defining clauses. Always default to "who" or "which" to maintain structural integrity.
